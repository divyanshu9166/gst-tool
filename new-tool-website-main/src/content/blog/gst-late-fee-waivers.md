Accumulating late fees on delayed GST returns can severely impact a small business's cash flow. If you've been putting off filing your returns because you're afraid of the mounting penalties, it's important to understand how the government caps these fees.

Before diving into waivers, you should know exactly what your current liability looks like. Use our [Late Fee Calculator](/late-fee-calculator) to get an estimate of your dues.

## The Maximum Late Fee Cap

Historically, late fees were allowed to compound indefinitely at ₹50 per day (or ₹20 for NIL returns) until they reached a theoretical maximum of ₹10,000 (₹5,000 CGST + ₹5,000 SGST). 

To provide relief to small taxpayers, the GST Council introduced rationalized maximum caps based on the Annual Aggregate Turnover (AATO):

1. **NIL Returns (No Tax Liability)**: Maximum late fee is capped at ₹500 (₹250 CGST + ₹250 SGST) per return.
2. **Turnover up to ₹1.5 Crore**: Maximum late fee is capped at ₹2,000 per return.
3. **Turnover between ₹1.5 Crore and ₹5 Crore**: Maximum late fee is capped at ₹5,000 per return.
4. **Turnover above ₹5 Crore**: The old cap of ₹10,000 per return remains applicable.

*(Note: These caps generally apply to GSTR-3B and GSTR-1. GSTR-9 annual returns have different capping rules based on a percentage of turnover).*

## What About the 18% Interest?

It is crucial to understand that while late *fees* are capped, **interest is never capped**. 
Under Section 50 of the CGST Act, 18% per annum interest is levied on the net tax liability paid in cash for every single day of delay. There are rarely any waivers for interest. You can compute this exact interest amount using our [Late Fee Calculator](/late-fee-calculator).

## GST Amnesty Schemes

Occasionally, the government announces "Amnesty Schemes" to encourage non-compliant taxpayers to clear their backlog. 
- During an amnesty scheme, the maximum late fee cap is drastically reduced (e.g., to ₹500 for normal returns and ₹0 for NIL returns) for a specific filing window.
- If you have years of pending returns, an amnesty scheme is the best time to clean up your [GST Due Dates Calendar](/gst-due-dates) slate.
- However, amnesty schemes are temporary and unpredictable. Relying on them is not a sustainable business strategy.
