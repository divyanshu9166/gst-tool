One of the most common misconceptions among newly registered businesses in India is that if they have no sales or purchases in a given month, they do not need to file a GST return. **This is completely false.** 

If you hold an active GSTIN, you are legally obligated to file your returns, even if all fields are zero. This is known as a "NIL Return." Failing to file it will trigger penalties, which you can estimate using our [Late Fee Calculator](/late-fee-calculator).

## What Qualifies as a NIL Return?

To file a NIL GSTR-3B for a specific tax period, all the following conditions must be met:
- You made no outward supplies (no sales).
- You received no inward supplies (no purchases).
- You do not have any reverse charge liability.
- You do not intend to claim any Input Tax Credit (ITC) for that period.
- You have no late fees to pay from previous months.

If you meet these criteria, you can file a NIL return via SMS or the [official GST Portal](https://www.gst.gov.in/) in under a minute.

## The Penalty for Not Filing a NIL Return

Even though your tax liability is zero, the government penalizes non-compliance. If you miss the deadline marked on your [Due Dates Calendar](/gst-due-dates), late fees start accruing immediately.

- **Late Fee Rate**: ₹20 per day (₹10 CGST + ₹10 SGST).
- **Maximum Cap**: The late fee for a NIL return is capped at a maximum of ₹500 (₹250 CGST + ₹250 SGST) per return.

While ₹500 might not seem like much, if you neglect to file NIL returns for 6 months, you will accumulate ₹3,000 in penalties. Furthermore, consecutive non-filing for 6 months gives the tax officer the authority to cancel your GST registration *suo moto* (on their own motion). 

## How to Stay Compliant

1. **Set Reminders**: Use our [GST Due Dates](/gst-due-dates) page to set monthly calendar alerts.
2. **File via SMS**: The government has introduced an SMS facility. Sending a simple SMS from your registered mobile number can file your NIL GSTR-3B and GSTR-1 instantly.
3. **Validate Business Operations**: If your business operations have ceased permanently, it is better to surrender/cancel your GST registration rather than endlessly filing NIL returns. (Check the active status of any business using a [GSTIN Validator](/gstin-validator)).

Never ignore a NIL return. Filing it on time is free, but forgetting it will cost you!
