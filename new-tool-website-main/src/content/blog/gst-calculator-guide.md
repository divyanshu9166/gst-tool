Calculating the Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a routine task for business owners, accountants, and even consumers in India. Whether you are generating an invoice or trying to figure out the base price of a product, understanding how to use a [GST Calculator](/gst-calculator) is essential.

## The Indian GST Structure

Before diving into the calculations, it is important to know that India follows a multi-tier tax structure. The standard GST rates are **5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%**, depending on the type of goods or services. 

When a transaction occurs within the same state (Intra-state), the GST is split equally into:
- **CGST (Central GST)**: Collected by the Central Government.
- **SGST (State GST)**: Collected by the State Government.

For inter-state transactions, a single **IGST (Integrated GST)** is levied, which goes entirely to the Central Government.

## How to Add GST to a Base Price

If you have the base price (excluding tax) and need to find the final price (inclusive of tax), the formula is straightforward:

**GST Amount** = (Base Price × GST Rate) / 100
**Total Price** = Base Price + GST Amount

### Example
You are selling a laptop with a base price of ₹40,000. The applicable GST rate is 18%.
- GST Amount = (40,000 × 18) / 100 = ₹7,200
- Total Price = 40,000 + 7,200 = ₹47,200

*Tip: If you are selling within the same state, this ₹7,200 is split into ₹3,600 CGST and ₹3,600 SGST.*

## How to Remove GST (Reverse Calculation)

Often, a product's MRP already includes the GST. If you need to find the pre-tax base price, you must perform a reverse calculation. You cannot simply subtract the percentage.

**Base Price** = (Total Price × 100) / (100 + GST Rate)
**GST Amount** = Total Price - Base Price

### Example
A mobile phone is sold for ₹15,000 (inclusive of 12% GST). What is the base price?
- Base Price = (15,000 × 100) / (100 + 12) = 1,500,000 / 112 = ₹13,392.85
- GST Amount = 15,000 - 13,392.85 = ₹1,607.15
